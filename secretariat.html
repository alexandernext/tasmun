Kristin C. and Lauren H.

Co-Secretary-Generals

Dear participants of TASMUN XII,

We are your Secretaries-Generals, Lauren and Kristin. Having both started our MUN journeys at TAS in the seventh grade, we have been able to witness the program flourish, providing valuable learning experiences for the students of our community. We are honored to be serving as the Secretaries-Generals for this year’s conference and we hope to put back into this program what we have gained from it.
From US Congressman John Lewis’s last letter, written shortly before his passing and published on the day of his funeral, he chose to use his last words to send a message to the future generation. John Lewis called upon readers to continue his life’s work of fighting for justice and equality, saying “Now it is your turn to let freedom ring.” John Lewis has always been a symbol of equality, from when he was the youthful leading figure of the civil rights movement to his career in Congress where he continued to fight for the rights of many. His life’s work was dedicated to combating inequalities and never did he lose this sense of direction. He embodies the essence of justice and inclusion. We drew inspiration from Lewis’s lasting and memorable words to build our conference theme.
This year, the theme we chose was “Passing the Torch of Inclusion and Justice.” The torch is symbolic of the responsibility of advancing the cause of equality and the hope of our new generation. This idea of handing off the torch to the ambitious future generation particularly resonates with us, as it channels the purpose of why we hold our TASMUN conferences annually: to foster a new generation of promising problem solvers that want to make a positive difference in our world.
We look forward to seeing all of you at our conference and are excited to see the future generation at work.

Best regards,
Kristin C. and Lauren H.
